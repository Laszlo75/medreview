# SPIRIT 2025 — clinical trial protocols

Source: Chan et al., *BMJ* 2025;389:e081477 (updated, harmonised with
CONSORT 2025). SPIRIT applies to **protocols** of trials, not final reports —
use `CONSORT.md` for completed-trial manuscripts. Reviewer: mark met /
partial / unmet with location. Items mirror CONSORT where possible; the
protocol-specific emphases are noted.

## Administrative / open science
- Title identifies the study as a trial protocol and the design.
- Trial registration: registry, ID, and that registration is/was prospective.
- Where the protocol and **statistical analysis plan** are/will be available.
- Data-sharing plan (de-identified participant data, code, materials).
- Funding sources and role of funders; author conflicts of interest.
- Roles and responsibilities: sponsor, contributors, committees (steering,
  data-monitoring), and their independence.

## Introduction
- Background and rationale, including why a trial is needed now and summary of
  known benefits/harms.
- Specific objectives / hypotheses.
- Trial design: type, framework (superiority/equivalence/non-inferiority),
  allocation ratio.

## Methods — participants, interventions, outcomes
- Study setting and list of sites.
- Eligibility criteria (participants; and sites/deliverers if applicable).
- Interventions and comparator with enough detail to replicate; criteria for
  modifying/discontinuing; adherence strategies; concomitant care allowed.
- Primary and secondary **outcomes** fully specified (variable, metric,
  aggregation, time point) with rationale.
- Participant timeline (schedule of enrolment, interventions, assessments —
  a figure is recommended).
- **Sample size** with all assumptions; recruitment strategy to reach it.

## Methods — assignment & blinding
- Allocation: sequence generation, concealment mechanism, implementation.
- Blinding: who is blinded and how; procedure for unblinding if needed.

## Methods — data & analysis
- Data collection, management, and plans to promote retention / handle
  missing data.
- Statistical methods for primary and secondary outcomes; additional analyses
  (subgroup, sensitivity), prespecified; analysis populations defined.
- How **harms** will be assessed and analysed.
- Interim analyses and stopping guidelines; data-monitoring committee.

## Ethics & dissemination
- Ethics approval status and plans for protocol amendments.
- Consent process and who obtains it; assent where relevant.
- Confidentiality and data-governance arrangements.
- Ancillary and post-trial care; access to the intervention after the trial.
- Dissemination policy (publication, data, participants, public).
- Patient/public involvement in design, conduct, reporting.
- Availability of the full protocol, dataset, and code.
