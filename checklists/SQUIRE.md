# SQUIRE 2.0 — quality improvement, audit & service evaluation

Source: Ogrinc et al., *BMJ Qual Saf* 2016;25:986–992. 18 items. Use for QI
projects, clinical audit write-ups, and service-evaluation reports (directly
relevant to utilisation/governance and audit-lead work). SQUIRE is
deliberately flexible — not every item fits every project; mark n-a where so.
Reviewer: mark met / partial / unmet / n-a with location.

## Title & abstract
- **1** — Title indicates this concerns improvement in healthcare (naming the
  aim, context, or method).
- **2** — Abstract summarises the problem, methods, interventions, and results.

## Introduction (why did you start?)
- **3** — Problem description: nature and significance of the local problem.
- **4** — Available knowledge: what's already known, including any formal
  frameworks or prior evidence.
- **5** — Rationale: the reasoning/theory or logic model for why the
  intervention was expected to work. *(Distinctive to QI — often missing.)*
- **6** — Specific aim(s) of the project.

## Methods (what did you do?)
- **7** — Context: key contextual elements (the setting, and factors that
  might affect generalisability).
- **8** — Intervention(s) described in enough detail to replicate, and who was
  involved.
- **9** — Study of the intervention: approach used to assess whether the
  intervention (not other factors) caused the change (e.g. **SPC / run charts /
  interrupted time series**).
- **10** — Measures chosen; their operational definitions; validity,
  reliability, and how completeness/accuracy were assessed.
- **11** — Analysis: qualitative and quantitative methods; for time-series,
  the **statistical process control** approach and how special-cause
  variation was distinguished from noise. *(Your XmR/EWMA/CUSUM territory —
  check the control limits and rules are appropriate.)*
- **12** — Ethical considerations: governance, consent/waiver, conflicts, and
  how ethical issues (including that this is QI vs research) were addressed.

## Results (what did you find?)
- **13** — Results: evolution of the intervention (including PDSA cycles),
  process and outcome measures over time (**annotated time-series charts**),
  unintended consequences, and any association between intervention and
  outcomes with contextual explanation. Details of missing data.

## Discussion (what does it mean?)
- **14** — Summary: key findings, strengths, relation to the rationale/aims.
- **15** — Interpretation: nature of the association; comparison with other
  evidence; impact on people and systems; reasons for any difference between
  observed and expected; costs/opportunity costs.
- **16** — Limitations: to internal validity (confounders), generalisability,
  and efforts to minimise/adjust for them.
- **17** — Conclusions: usefulness, sustainability, potential for spread, and
  implications for practice and further study.

## Other
- **18** — Funding and role of funders.
