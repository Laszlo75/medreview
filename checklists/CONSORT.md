# CONSORT 2025 — randomised trial reports

Source: Hopewell et al., *BMJ / JAMA / Lancet / Nat Med / PLoS Med* 2025
(PLoS Med 22(4):e1004587, CC-BY). 30 items. **Supersedes CONSORT 2010** —
do not use the old version. Focuses on two-group parallel trials; specialised
designs need the relevant extension. Reviewer: mark met / partial / unmet with
location. The new open-science section (items 2–5) and items 8, 15, 21b/c,
24 are the 2025 additions and are commonly missing.

## Title & abstract
- **1a** — Identified as a randomised trial in the title.
- **1b** — Structured abstract of design, methods, results, conclusions.

## Open science (new section)
- **2** — Trial registry name, ID (with URL), and date of registration.
- **3** — Where the protocol and **statistical analysis plan** can be accessed.
- **4** — Where/how de-identified participant data (with data dictionary),
  statistical code, and other materials can be accessed.
- **5a** — Funding sources and role of funders in design/conduct/analysis/
  reporting.
- **5b** — Financial and other conflicts of interest of the authors.

## Introduction
- **6** — Scientific background and rationale.
- **7** — Specific objectives, related to benefits **and harms**.

## Methods
- **8** — Patient/public involvement in design, conduct, reporting.
- **9** — Trial design, including type (parallel, crossover…), allocation
  ratio, and framework (superiority / equivalence / non-inferiority).
- **10** — Important changes after commencement, including any outcomes or
  analyses that were not prespecified, with reasons.
- **11** — Settings and locations.
- **12a** — Eligibility criteria for participants.
- **12b** — If applicable, eligibility for sites and for those delivering the
  intervention.
- **13** — Interventions and comparator with enough detail to replicate
  (where to find manuals etc.).
- **14** — Prespecified primary and secondary outcomes: measurement variable,
  analysis metric, aggregation method, and time point.
- **15** — How **harms** were defined and assessed.
- **16a** — How sample size was determined, with assumptions.
- **16b** — Interim analyses and stopping guidelines.
- **17a** — Who generated the allocation sequence and the method.
- **17b** — Type of randomisation; restriction (stratification, blocking,
  block size).
- **18** — Allocation-concealment mechanism and steps to conceal until
  assignment.
- **19** — Implementation: who enrolled and who assigned participants.
- **20a** — Who was blinded (participants, providers, assessors, analysts).
- **20b** — If blinded, how, and similarity of interventions.
- **21a** — Statistical methods comparing groups for primary/secondary
  outcomes, including harms.
- **21b** — Who is included in each analysis and in which group (e.g. all
  randomised).
- **21c** — How **missing data** were handled.
- **21d** — Additional analyses (subgroup, sensitivity), prespecified vs
  post hoc.

## Results
- **22a** — Numbers randomised, receiving intended intervention, analysed for
  the primary outcome, per group (flow diagram).
- **22b** — Losses and exclusions after randomisation, with reasons.
- **23a** — Dates of recruitment and follow-up.
- **23b** — Why the trial ended or stopped, if relevant.
- **24a** — Intervention/comparator **as actually administered** (who
  delivered, adherence, fidelity).
- **24b** — Concomitant care during the trial.
- **25** — Baseline demographic and clinical characteristics per group.
- **26** — For each primary/secondary outcome, per group: number analysed,
  number with available data at the time point, result and estimated effect
  size with precision (95% CI); for binary outcomes, absolute **and** relative
  effect sizes.
- **27** — All harms/unintended events per group.
- **28** — Any other analyses (subgroup, sensitivity), prespecified vs post hoc.

## Discussion
- **29** — Interpretation consistent with results, balancing benefits and
  harms, in the light of other evidence.
- **30** — Limitations: sources of bias, imprecision, generalisability, and
  multiplicity.
