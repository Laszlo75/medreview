# STROBE — observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional)

Source: von Elm et al., *Ann Intern Med / BMJ / PLoS Med* 2007. 22 items.
Reviewer: for each item mark met / partial / unmet / n-a with location.
Where an item differs by design, the (c)ohort, case-(cc)ontrol and
(cs)cross-sectional variants are noted.

## Title & abstract
- **1a** — Study design stated in the title or abstract with a common term.
- **1b** — Abstract gives a balanced, informative summary of what was done and
  found.

## Introduction
- **2** — Scientific background and rationale reported.
- **3** — Specific objectives, including any prespecified hypotheses.

## Methods
- **4** — Key elements of study design stated early in the paper.
- **5** — Setting, locations, and relevant dates (recruitment, exposure,
  follow-up, data collection).
- **6a** — Eligibility criteria; sources and methods of selecting participants.
  (cohort) methods of follow-up; (cc) case ascertainment and control
  selection with rationale; (cs) selection method.
- **6b** — (cohort) matching criteria and numbers exposed/unexposed;
  (cc) matching criteria and controls-per-case.
- **7** — All variables defined: outcomes, exposures, predictors, potential
  confounders, effect modifiers; diagnostic criteria given.
- **8** — For each variable, sources of data and details of measurement;
  comparability of assessment across groups.
- **9** — Efforts to address potential sources of **bias**. *(High yield —
  often skipped.)*
- **10** — How **study size** was arrived at.
- **11** — How quantitative variables were handled; groupings and why.
- **12a** — All statistical methods, including those for confounding.
- **12b** — Methods for subgroups and interactions.
- **12c** — How **missing data** were addressed. *(High yield.)*
- **12d** — (cohort) how loss to follow-up was addressed; (cc) matching of
  cases/controls in analysis; (cs) sampling-strategy accounting.
- **12e** — Sensitivity analyses.

## Results
- **13a** — Numbers at each stage (eligible → examined → eligible →
  confirmed → included → analysed); a flow diagram is encouraged.
- **13b** — Reasons for non-participation at each stage.
- **14a** — Characteristics of participants; information on exposures and
  confounders.
- **14b** — Number of participants with missing data per variable.
- **14c** — (cohort) summarise follow-up time.
- **15** — Outcome events or summary measures (by group / over time as
  appropriate).
- **16a** — Unadjusted and confounder-adjusted estimates with precision (95%
  CI); which confounders were adjusted and why.
- **16b** — Category boundaries when continuous variables were categorised.
- **16c** — If relevant, absolute risk translated for a meaningful time period.
- **17** — Other analyses done (subgroups, interactions, sensitivity).

## Discussion
- **18** — Key results with reference to objectives.
- **19** — **Limitations**, direction and magnitude of potential bias.
  *(High yield — must be honest and specific.)*
- **20** — Cautious overall interpretation considering objectives, limitations,
  multiplicity, and other evidence.
- **21** — Generalisability (external validity).

## Other
- **22** — Funding and role of funders; where the original study protocol can
  be accessed if relevant.
