# STARD 2015 — diagnostic accuracy studies

Source: Bossuyt et al., *BMJ* 2015;351:h5527. 30 items. Reviewer: mark
met / partial / unmet with location.

## Title/abstract
- **1** — Identified as a study of diagnostic accuracy using at least one
  measure of accuracy (e.g. sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV).
- **2** — Structured abstract of design, methods, results, conclusions.

## Introduction
- **3** — Scientific and clinical background, and intended use / clinical role
  of the index test.
- **4** — Study objectives and hypotheses.

## Methods
- **5** — Whether data collection was prospective or retrospective.
- **6** — Eligibility criteria.
- **7** — How potentially eligible participants were identified (presenting
  symptoms, prior tests, the registry/database).
- **8** — Where and when participants were identified (setting, dates).
- **9** — Whether participants were a consecutive/random/convenience series.
- **10a** — Index test, in enough detail to replicate.
- **10b** — Reference standard, in enough detail to replicate.
- **11** — Rationale for choosing that reference standard.
- **12a** — Definition of and rationale for index-test **cut-offs/categories**,
  distinguishing prespecified from exploratory.
- **12b** — Same for the reference standard.
- **13a** — Whether index-test readers were **blinded** to the reference
  standard. *(High yield — a core bias.)*
- **13b** — Whether reference-standard assessors were blinded to the index
  test.
- **14** — Methods for estimating/comparing measures of accuracy.
- **15** — How **indeterminate** index-test / reference-standard results were
  handled.
- **16** — How **missing data** on index test and reference standard were
  handled.
- **17** — Any analyses of variability in accuracy (prespecified vs
  exploratory).
- **18** — Intended **sample size** and how determined.

## Results
- **19** — Flow of participants (a diagram is strongly recommended).
- **20** — Baseline demographic and clinical characteristics.
- **21a** — Distribution of severity of disease in those with the target
  condition.
- **21b** — Distribution of alternative diagnoses in those without it.
- **22** — Time interval and any interventions between index test and
  reference standard. *(Disease-progression / partial-verification bias.)*
- **23** — Cross-tabulation of index-test results (or their distribution) by
  the reference standard.
- **24** — Estimates of diagnostic accuracy with **precision (95% CI)**.
- **25** — Any adverse events from index test or reference standard.

## Discussion
- **26** — Study limitations, including sources of potential bias, statistical
  uncertainty, and generalisability.
- **27** — Implications for practice, including intended clinical role.

## Other
- **28** — Registration number and registry.
- **29** — Where the full study protocol can be accessed.
- **30** — Sources of funding and role of funders.
